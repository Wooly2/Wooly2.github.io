Apple

Apples are a good source of dietary fiber.

The crabapple is the only apple native to North America.

Apples come in thousands of varieties.

Durian

The durian is known for its strong odor, often described as a mix of gym socks and onions.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often called the "king of fruits."

Despite its strong smell, durian is loved by many for its unique taste and creamy texture.

Lychee

Lychee is a small, round fruit with a rough skin and sweet, juicy flesh.

It originated in China and is now grown in various parts of the world.

Lychee is often used in desserts, drinks, and as a topping for salads and ice creams.

Mango

Mangoes are often called the "king of fruits" in many tropical regions.

They are rich in vitamins A and C and are a good source of dietary fiber.

Mangoes are widely used in various cuisines and are enjoyed in both sweet and savory dishes.

Dragon Fruit

Dragon fruit, also known as pitaya, is a tropical fruit with a vibrant pink or yellow skin.

It has a mild, sweet taste and is packed with antioxidants and vitamin C.

The fruit's flesh is dotted with tiny edible seeds and is often used in smoothies and desserts.
